Continuity Notes-May 26

Dora: Rien que Moins

Synopsis

TED Labruyère, 18, fresh out of high school, struggles to find a job. With long hair and delicate features, he faces the awkward situation being too delicate for labourer work. At an employment agency, a clerk suggests an unusual opportunity. She describes the position as being a staff member of a “historical fair”. Despite initial hesitation due to being a fair distance away, Ted finally agrees to the offer and signs a contract, drawn by the promise of employment and adventure.

Key Plot Points and Details

Protagonist’s Transformation:

  • Beige Leather Bag: Given to the protagonist by his aunt, used as her purse for the last two years.

  • Aunt’s Rebuke: The aunt previously rebuked the protagonist for being “ungrateful,” leading to his reluctance to protest the bag.

Travel and Setting:

  • VW Combivan: The protagonist is traveling with a group of young women to a historical faire in a VW combivan.

  • Historical Faire: The setting is a modern-day “Dickens Faire” where participants perform roles from the 1780s.

  • Road Trip Details: The group is traveling from Perth to New South Wales, with Kathy driving and guiding them.

  • Trip to Thornton: This is phase one of the story.

  • Main Setting: The bulk of the events occur at an exact replica of the Château de La Chapelle Gauthier, set in the New South Wales hinterland forests in Australia. The replica has a very weathered appearance from the outside but is beautifully furnished for the female members of the inhabitants (the girls and Miss Kathy).

  • Faire/Retreat: The ‘faire’ is more of a retreat for those who wish to enjoy an authentic 18th-century experience.

Group Dynamics:

  • Young Women’s Conversation: The women discuss their excitement and plans for the faire, including costumes, wigs, dances, and historical experiences.

  • Protagonist’s Discomfort: Highlight the protagonist’s blush and discomfort when teased about carrying a feminine bag.

Historical Context:

  • Common Foods of the 1780s: Bread, porridge, root vegetables, legumes, dairy products, pork, fish, eggs, seasonal fruits, and herbs.

  • Château Information: The Château de La Chapelle Gauthier, used in the film “Portrait de la Jeune Fille en Feu,” was built in the 17th century and is located in Seine-et-Marne, near Paris.

Character Development / Supporting Characters:

  • Kathy dit de Bonne Chasse: Also known as Miss Kathy. She is of French extraction, speaks French and English, and has nobility in her ancestry.

  • Lucy: Gay and a feminist.

  • Chloe: Lucy’s love interest.

  • Emma: Raised on a farm and a cowgirl.

  • Jessica: Understands and enjoys writing and history.

  • Sarah: From the UK, with a very strong accent and acerbic wit.

  • Pauline: The maid-servant. She is also from France, speaks mostly French and a bit of broken English, has strong maternal instincts, is usually very quiet but can be quite firm and even stern if circumstances warrant it. She follows Miss Kathy’s mandates closely and shares many of her proclivities.

Protagonist’s Repeated Phrase:

  • Phrase: “You’ll get used to it.”

  • Context: Pauline says this to Ted whenever he faces a new challenge. At the end, Emma, his spouse, will say these words at a significant juncture.

Main Character (DORA, née TED):

  • Description: An 18-year-old petite, slender, timid androgynous male who is not robust. He is straight, naive, trusting, obedient, sensitive, and honourable.

  • Background: Academically poor except for music and history, he is jobless, kicked out of the home, and has no strong social ties.

  • Skills: Composer of classical music and speaks French, being somewhat conversant.

  • Personality: He is a perfectionist in anything he does.

  • Adventure: He will encounter significant gender-challenging adventures.

  • Weakness: To be determined.
