## Context summary — Wardrobe scaling, Sharl’s “wife-work,” and the 1770s visual direction

### Where the story is now

* We’re in **early-to-mid Act II**: Wardrobe has proven viability, shipped a demanding **200-unit wholesale order**, and survived — but the cost (fatigue, overtime, lost study hours, safety risk) makes it clear the operation must **scale sustainably** or risk becoming exploitative.
* Core ethos remains: **women-led competence network**, governance by **systems + written terms**, no romance fog, no fetish optics. Work is moral, daylight-proof, and *visible*.

### Character dynamics and emotional hinge

* **Celeste** has moved from “aloof curiosity” into **open stake**: she admits to Sharl that she doesn’t just like him — it’s *more*, though she’s not ready to name the final word. She keeps it clean: no coercion, no entitlement, no “tests,” just clarity and boundaries.
* **Sharl** continues to embody “wife-work” (support infrastructure, quiet competence, logging rather than performing). His provider reflex still flickers, but he now **logs it** rather than acting it out.
* **Sarah** remains a beloved “loose cannon”: piquant, funny, sharp, but **not romantic** toward Sharl. She adds flavour and pressure-tests culture without becoming the plot.
* **Tilly** becomes the new “interest vector”: she misreads Celeste’s restraint as Sharl’s availability. Her mild, deniable attention triggers **Celeste’s jealousy** (not possessive ownership — a provider’s fear of ambiguity and drift). Tilly is status-conscious and easily corrected; she won’t jeopardize her role.

### Key scene(s) created in this block

* **“Two Inches Too Close” (Celeste POV):**
  Tilly drifts into deniable intimacy while Sharl is wear-testing a revised chemise. Celeste corrects culture cleanly (“Sharl isn’t a focus group; he’s QA”), then privately tells Sharl she’s not indifferent — she’s been restrained to avoid entitlement, but restraint reads as indifference to others. She admits it’s **more than liking**, and insists he doesn’t have to “provide” to be worthy of her. Ends with: *keep it clean, then back to work.*

### Patriarchy theme integration

* We explicitly avoid the “cheap” move where Sharl experiences women erasing his accomplishments. Instead, Wardrobe’s women **credit talent properly** — they name labour and refuse nullification.
* A proposed thematic mirror to *Portrait de la Jeune Fille en Feu*: **girls’ night in** where Celeste, Lauren, Camille (French friend), and Sharl watch the film. Discussion of **Orpheus** as entitlement/possession disguised as devotion; Sharl reacts with justice-oriented disgust at patriarchal norms. Celeste and Lauren note parallels to Sharl’s reluctance to claim credit, and Celeste resolves to make his authorship visible in ethical, non-theatrical ways (e.g., his name on the chemise/product page).

### Operations / scaling direction

* Post-200 order “post-mortem” beat:
  Celeste and Mara treat the shipment as a success **and** a warning. Decision: upscale with roles, not heroics:

  * add **production stitchers**
  * a **finishing specialist**
  * expanded **fulfilment/packing**
  * capacity rules: price/lead time staged, no martyr-maths, no invisible labour
  * protect Celeste’s **education hours** as non-negotiable.

### Visual / image generation direction (1770s)

* We refined prompts toward **1770s** authenticity and away from “storybook / folk / Victorian maid” drift.
* Strong “era magnets” used: **robe à l’anglaise, back pleats, stomacher, fichu, apron, petticoat**, documentary realism.
* Observed issue: AI tends to produce “button-front bodice dress” and high-neck severity, reading as **puritan/colonial**.
* Fixes recommended:

  * emphasize **open front edges with robings + clearly visible pinned stomacher**
  * avoid **visible buttons/lacing**
  * soften styling: **mob cap** often reads less severe than kerchief
  * add negatives: **no button placket, no lace-up bodice, not Victorian, not colonial/puritan, no high neck**.
* Current generated images show strong progress: the second image reads more 18th-century due to square neckline + shift ruffle; next step is forcing the closure to stop drifting into buttoned bodice.

### Possible future format shift

* Because the work has been built scene-by-scene with clear beats, we noted the project could be **adapted into a screenplay** later without major structural pain: each scene already has a purpose, conflict, and turn.
