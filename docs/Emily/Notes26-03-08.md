## Your current passage, with the logic adjusted

Here is a version very close to yours, just nudged into a stronger emotional lane:

> Charli grimaced. “Brittany reads me like a book, Celeste. And then, when I let slip that I had started at the Faire in maintenance, oh sheesh. I can’t believe I just blabbered that out.”

## Why that version works better

Because now Celeste is doing what only she can do:

* slowing Charli down
* putting the facts in order
* preventing an impulsive disclosure
* giving Charli back a sense of agency

That is much more satisfying than a quick yes/no answer.

## A few little line notes

### This is almost right:

> One side of her mouth squeezed as her eyebrows rose.

“Squeezed” is not impossible, but it feels a touch awkward on the mouth.

You might try:

* One side of her mouth pulled tight as her eyebrows rose.
* Her mouth pinched to one side as her eyebrows lifted.
* One corner of her mouth tightened.

### This is good but can be a hair cleaner:

> "I dunno." She shrugged.

Perfectly fine. Or:

* “I dunno.” Her shrug looked helpless.

if you want a tiny extra note.

### This needs smoothing:

> She scratched her head.

It’s okay, though a little neutral/general. You might get more from:

* She rubbed at the back of her head.
* Her hand went to her hair.
* She scratched lightly at her scalp.

But this is minor.

## My honest recommendation

I would **not** keep Celeste saying, essentially, *yes, tell Brittany*.

I would have her say something more like:

* **Not tonight.**
* **Not because you’re scared.**
* **Not unless you want to.**
* **Not before she’s even asked.**

That preserves both women:

* Charli remains frightened but not foolish
* Celeste remains protective and intelligent rather than simplistic

And it gives the scene somewhere to go next.

## The strongest single line available to you here

If I had to pick one, it might be:

> **“You tell people when you choose, Charli. Not when panic corners you.”**

That is clean, memorable, and very Celeste.

Or, a slightly softer variant:

> **“Not like that, petal. Not because you’re frightened.”**

Both are strong.

You are very close here. The scene is not failing at all — it has simply reached the point where its ethics and emotional intelligence have to become precise. That is why it suddenly feels difficult, and it’s also why it matters 💛
