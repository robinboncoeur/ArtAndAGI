👌 Absolutely, Robyn. That beat spine is *gold* — and it plays perfectly with your “ladder” rule. 🧵✨

A tiny tweak that can make it even more *usable* in the moment: attach a **one-line job** (and an “altitude target”) to each beat, so you’re never tempted to drift into Thesis.

### Beat jobs + ladder targets

**Beat 1 — Entry**

* **Job:** establish *where we are* + Celeste’s baseline body state.
* **Ladder:** 1–2 (sensory + body)

**Beat 2 — Inciting Shift (PRIMARY)**

* **Job:** the *first* thing that changes the weather (Charli’s call → male voice → spike).
* **Ladder:** 1–2, with a single 3 if needed (annoyance/flush)

**Beat 3 — Internal Calibration**

* **Job:** Celeste *re-aims herself* (what she thinks is happening; what she decides to do with it).
* **Ladder:** 2–3, brief 4 allowed (one line, not a list)

**Beat 4 — Choice**

* **Job:** what Celeste *does* (tone choice: soothe, correct, tease, set boundary).
* **Ladder:** 2 (action + voice)

**Beat 5 — Power Adjustment (TURN)**

* **Job:** the dynamic shifts (Charli steadies; Celeste asserts; guilt flare; or a new thread: Brittany/maintenance reveal).
* **Ladder:** 1–3, with a clean “end-weight” sentence

**Beat 6 — Exit Note**

* **Job:** leave a hook or a resonance (promise, rule, tenderness, tiny humour).
* **Ladder:** 1–2 (image / gesture / line that lingers)

If you keep *Exit Note* down at rung 1–2, your scenes will end with that lovely aftertaste rather than commentary. 😊

When you draft Scene Four, you can literally paste those beat headers and write one paragraph per beat. Then we do a light pass: “Is each beat doing its job?” and “Did any rung 5 sneak in?”
