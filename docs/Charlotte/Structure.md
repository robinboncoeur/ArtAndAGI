### üß± Scene Structure Framework 101

*(Built specifically for your style and this story‚Äôs DNA)* This is not Hollywood three-act nonsense. This is micro-architecture for emotionally layered scenes.

---

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ The 6-Beat Scene Spine

Every scene ‚Äî even quiet ones ‚Äî should answer these six questions.

---

#### Beat 1 ‚Äî **Entry Position**

Where is everyone emotionally *before* the scene begins?  
Not plot. Not action.

**Emotional temperature.**

Example:

* Charli is steady but socially tired.
* Celeste is focused but slightly preoccupied.
* Sarah is sharp and watchful.

Write this for yourself in one sentence before drafting.

If you don‚Äôt know entry temperature, you will wander.

---

#### Beat 2 ‚Äî **Inciting Shift**

Something small but directional changes.

Not explosion.
Not drama.

A look.
A question.
An invitation.
A comment.
An absence.

Example:
‚ÄúPool party on Saturday.‚Äù

That‚Äôs it.

But now tension exists.

---

#### Beat 3 ‚Äî **Internal Calibration**

This is your signature strength.

The character interprets the shift.

Not philosophising.
Not essay-writing.

Just:

* What does this mean?
* What do I feel?
* What is at stake?

Keep it embodied.
Keep it sensory.
Keep it inside the body.

---

#### Beat 4 ‚Äî **Choice or Behaviour**

Someone acts.

Not necessarily boldly.

But something is chosen:

* Deflect.
* Accept.
* Change subject.
* Go quiet.
* Ask a question.

No scene without a choice.

---

#### Beat 5 ‚Äî **Power Adjustment**

After the behaviour, who holds more control now?

Even subtly.

Power doesn‚Äôt mean domination.
It means:
Who is steering?

If power does not shift, the scene is static.

---

#### Beat 6 ‚Äî **Exit Note**

End on:

* A line with weight.
* A withheld answer.
* A small physical gesture.
* A decision forming.

Never exit on summary.

Exit on tension.

---

### üîÑ The One-Sentence Rule

After drafting, you should be able to summarise the scene as:

‚ÄúIn this scene, ___ shifts because ___.‚Äù

If you can‚Äôt?
The scene lacks spine.

---

### ü™° For Your Specific Story

We add 3 recurring diagnostic layers.

---

#### A. Visibility Meter

In this scene:
Is Charli:

* Hidden?
* Seen?
* Misread?
* Learning?

Track it.

It‚Äôs her long arc.

---

#### B. Celeste‚Äôs Control Index

In this scene:
Is Celeste:

* Designing?
* Restraining?
* Reacting?
* Misjudging?
* Protecting?

If she is neutral for too long, she flattens.

---

#### C. Trust Expansion Check

Does this scene:

* Increase Charli‚Äôs reliance solely on Celeste?
* Or widen her trust network?

We want widening.

Consciously.

---

### üß† Avoiding the ‚ÄúWeeds‚Äù

Here‚Äôs where you get lost sometimes:

You start refining language before the structure is stable.

So here‚Äôs your new discipline:

1. Write ugly.
2. Confirm the six beats exist.
3. Confirm power shift.
4. Confirm arc relevance.
5. THEN polish.

Polishing before spine = swamp.

---

### üìê Scene Size Control

For your writing style:

* Intimate scene: 800‚Äì1500 words.
* Structural shift scene: 1200‚Äì2000.
* Quiet connector: 600‚Äì900.

If it exceeds 2500?
You‚Äôre likely blending two scenes.

Split.

---

### üß® What Makes a Scene Earn Its Place?

It must:

* Advance character.
* Or shift power.
* Or introduce new tension.
* Or resolve a prior tension.

If it only repeats emotional state?
Cut or compress.

---

### ü™û A Quick Example ‚Äî Pool Invitation Scene

Entry: Charli steady, Brittany relaxed.
Inciting shift: ‚ÄúBeach Saturday?‚Äù
Internal calibration: stomach tight, calculation begins.
Choice: ‚ÄúI might be busy.‚Äù
Power shift: Brittany senses something withheld.
Exit: ‚ÄúYou don‚Äôt like swimming, do you?‚Äù

Scene complete.

Next scene deals with fallout.

---

### üß≠ Advanced Layer (When You‚Äôre Ready)

You can also map scenes in waves:

* External challenge.
* Internal interpretation.
* Relational reaction.
* Skill acquisition.
* New equilibrium.

But 101 is enough for now.

---

### Final Important Note

Structure is not there to constrain you.

It is there so that when you write softly,
the softness has weight.

You already have emotional intelligence.
Now we give it scaffolding.

---
