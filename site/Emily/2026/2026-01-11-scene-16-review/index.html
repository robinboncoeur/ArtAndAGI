Where we are, structurally (so we aim the edits correctly)¶

- “You’re allowed to touch more because you don’t contaminate the work.”
- “You’re allowed to see more because you don’t gossip.”
- “You’re allowed to decide small things because you choose the standard, not your ego.”

What I’ll be looking for in Scene 17 (fast but sharp)¶

If you paste it, I’ll do a quick pass specifically on:

1) The test Mara sets (it should be concrete)¶

Rung 2 works best if Mara gives him a situation where: - there’s ambiguity (not just “do X”), - he can either choose the easy human impulse or the professional standard, - and Mara watches what he does without announcing she’s watching.

Examples of “Second Rung” tests: - He notices a flaw (strain line, gaping, twisting seam, puckering) and has to decide whether to hide it, mention it, or log it. - He’s given access to the ledger or fabric stash under supervision — and the test is whether he treats it like treasure or like inventory. - He has to interact with a staff member (or client) and hold to policy: no flirting, no boasting, no male-coded banter — just competence. - Someone makes a joke at his expense and he must not perform masculinity back at them (no “laugh it off like a bloke”), but also not collapse. He chooses neutral dignity.

2) The women’s authority should land through action, not declaration¶

Mara doesn’t say, “I’m in charge.” She: - allocates work, - sets constraints, - names what matters, - cuts off noise, - and moves on.

Celeste’s authority at this point should be more social governance than technical: - she steers the emotional room, - she decides what Charlie is for, - she corrects framing if someone tries to turn him into a spectacle.

Lauren, if present, is your “adult professional world” anchor: - calm, consequential, and practical, - a woman who’s seen men fail standards and isn’t impressed by potential.

3) Charlie’s internal tone: eager + careful + still slightly “male-incorrect”¶

Second rung Charlie is often: - trying hard to do well, - confused about what matters most, - tempted to seek reassurance, - and learning that reassurance isn’t the currency here.

He can want praise — but he shouldn’t get it.
He gets: “Good. Next.”

4) The “tick” that shows he’s moving up¶

I like to build Rung 2 around a single small moment that proves maturation. Eg: - He writes the defect down even though it makes him look bad. - He asks one precise question instead of five needy ones. - He corrects himself before being corrected. - He doesn’t perform when given slightly more responsibility.

Mara’s acknowledgement is minimal — often non-verbal: - a nod, - sliding a tool closer, - leaving him alone with something for thirty seconds longer than before, - using his name without edge, - or saying, “Again.”
