Celeste Story Canon Bible (Working Draft)¶

😄🧵💛👭 💞 🖤 🍓 🌶 🚪 🔑 🛋 🫧 🌩 🌧 🧵 🪡 👗 👚 👜 👠 🩰 💄 💋 🎻 📒 ✂ 🩸 💧 🚺 —

Purpose¶

This is a working canon bible for Robyn’s story world, primarily covering Celeste’s Girl and The Deep End, with emphasis on:
- locked continuity
- character truths
- relational dynamics
- thematic guardrails
- scene-to-scene continuity
- open questions that should not yet be treated as settled canon

This document distinguishes between:
- Locked canon — established and safe to treat as true
- Provisional canon — likely true / currently preferred, but still revisable
- Open question — deliberately unresolved

1. Core Story Identity¶

1.1 Project overview¶

Locked canon

Robyn is building a women-led story world centred on Celeste, Charli, and the wider female structure around them. The work explores: - gendered social conditioning - female competence and authority - belonging, dignity, and emotional safety - womanhood as lived social reality rather than abstraction - young women shaping one another through standards, care, and intelligent pressure

The stories are not meant to be bleak for bleakness’ sake. They aim to be: - emotionally truthful - reader-protective without being dishonest - hopeful without sentimentality - clear-eyed about risk without becoming despair-driven

1.2 Moral / tonal contract with the reader¶

Locked canon

The project’s governing promise is approximately: - the story does not deny that the world contains cruelty, danger, and social risk - the story does not surrender to fear as ultimate truth - the story does not humiliate vulnerable characters for hurting - the story aims to leave the reader steadier, clearer, and less ashamed

The work should model what a sane, loving, non-panicked female response sounds like.

1.3 General thematic centre¶

Locked canon

The stories privilege: - women as the stronger, deciding, structuring forces - female social intelligence - standards over sentimentality - protection through competence, order, and earned trust - transformation that is guided, not chaotic

Wardrobe and related women-led environments are not merely accepting spaces; they are containers of female governance and competence.

2. World and Setting¶

2.1 Wardrobe¶

Locked canon

Wardrobe is a women-led costume / craft / social environment shaped by: - standards - competence - practical intelligence - female hierarchy without pettiness - belonging conditional on behaviour and reliability

It functions as more than a workplace. It is: - a social structure - a training ground - a place of containment and placement - a women’s competence network

2.2 Geography¶

Locked canon

  • Wardrobe proper is in Victoria, off the Great Ocean Road.
  • The newer Faire / new site connected to The Deep End is in Queensland, near Maleny on the Sunshine Coast.
  • Earlier conceptions placing it on the Gold Coast have been superseded.

2.3 The new Faire / Queensland site¶

Locked canon

The Queensland site provides a new social environment in which Charli must function without the full day-to-day protective enclosure of Wardrobe.

This matters because: - Charli is now physically away from Celeste and the full women-led container - she must socially function in a new female group - ordinary young-woman situations become practical tests of belonging

3. Timeline Overview¶

3.1 Broad project structure¶

Provisional canon

  • Celeste’s Girl establishes Charli / Charlie’s earlier transformation arc and the central Celeste–Charli dynamic.
  • The Deep End expands outward into a new environment where Charli must function more independently.
  • Possible later books continue into broader adulthood / business / city / deeper relational structures.

3.2 Immediate current timeline in The Deep End¶

Locked / current working canon

Current discussion has reached: - Charli has arrived / settled into the Queensland setting. - The first week has passed relatively uneventfully. - Charli is now in teaching mode and loves it. - She is respected by those around her, including Fiona. - Sarah has been busy with finishing details of Wardrobe’s physical plant and has not been in Charli’s immediate orbit. - Brittany has remained observant, noticing things that do not wholly reconcile with “tomboy,” but has not probed further. - A major short phone scene with Celeste has just occurred, stabilising Charli and reaffirming their bond. - Scene Five is expected to move to Friday night, where the girls suggest a pool party. - Scene Six is expected to centre on Charli’s possible selective disclosure to Brittany, provided Brittany earns that trust in Scene Five.

4. Principal Character Canon¶

4.1 Charli / Charlie / Charli Rossignol¶

Locked canon

Core identity¶

Charli is a young woman whose life has been shaped by a complex process of feminisation, social realignment, and female guidance.

She is: - petite - physically not strong - emotionally vulnerable but not weak in spirit - deeply responsive to female structure and approval - supportive rather than primary in energy - temperamentally aligned with womanhood - lacking some of the formative peer-group conditioning cis girls receive

Current social truth¶

Locked canon

Charli is now physically indistinguishable from a cis girl her age, even to sharp-eyed cis women. Her social risks are therefore those of other young women, but she does not possess their accumulated lived experience.

Psychological / emotional truth¶

Locked canon

Charli: - wants to belong properly - is highly responsive to female guidance - tends toward accommodation rather than assertion - can be observant and intelligent under pressure - is often frightened not because she is melodramatic, but because she notices real ambiguity and danger before it fully arrives - is capable, useful, and well-regarded when given a clear role

Current Deep End state¶

Locked canon

Charli is thriving in teaching mode and winning respect in the Queensland setting. However, she remains vulnerable in situations where ordinary girlhood becomes bodily / socially complicated.

Disclosure principle¶

Locked canon

Charli should not disclose private truths out of panic. If she discloses, it should be because another person has become someone she trusts.

4.2 Celeste¶

Locked canon

Core role¶

Celeste is one of the central governing intelligences of the story world.

She is: - stronger and more decisive than Charli - emotionally intelligent - clear-headed under distress - not sentimental in a sloppy way - capable of being protective, controlling, perceptive, and formative

Narrative importance¶

Locked canon

The story is, in a profound sense, at least as much about Celeste as it is about Charli. Her judgments, desires, interpretations, and structuring intelligence shape the emotional and moral atmosphere.

POV / voice rule¶

Locked canon

Celeste POV should be written in: - 1st person - past tense - crisp, non-explanatory, show-don’t-tell language

Functional story role¶

Locked canon

Celeste often provides: - accurate naming - distinction between uncertainty and danger - discernment rather than soothing cliché - female moral atmosphere

Current Deep End state¶

Locked canon

Celeste had earlier feared that Charli might not cope being away from the full Wardrobe container. The recent phone scene reassures Celeste that: - Charli can still be reached - their bond still steadies her - distance has changed the form of support, not erased it

Key relational truth¶

Locked canon

Celeste represents “home” to Charli, not merely in romance but in emotional shelter, orientation, and female placement.

4.3 Sarah¶

Locked canon

Sarah is: - acerbic - dry - standards-driven - sharp in wit - not generally over-demonstrative

Her tenderness, when it appears, is notable precisely because it emerges against this baseline.

Current Deep End role¶

Locked canon

Sarah has been preoccupied with the practical finishing touches of Wardrobe’s physical plant and has not recently been central in Charli’s day-to-day Queensland orbit.

Voice rule¶

Locked canon

Sarah’s voice should remain dry, sharp, and consistent even if the wider narrative tone later softens.

4.4 Brittany¶

Locked canon / emerging canon

Brittany is becoming a crucial figure in The Deep End.

Current function¶

She is: - observant - practical - grounded in real-world knowledge - perceptive without yet becoming invasive - capable of friendship based on mutual respect

Current dynamic with Charli¶

Locked canon

Charli admires Brittany for her practical competence and real-world knowledge. Their friendship is solidifying.

Brittany has noticed elements of Charli that do not fit a simple “tomboy” reading, but she has not pressed.

Desired narrative role¶

Locked canon for current planning

Brittany should: - show consistency and kindness - handle ambiguity with grace - avoid forcing disclosure - protect Charli’s dignity without making a show of it

If she does this, she earns Charli’s trust.

Scene Five / Six arc¶

Provisional canon but strongly preferred

  • In Scene Five, Brittany should notice Charli’s discomfort around the pool-party situation and help her out quietly, without fanfare.
  • In Scene Six, Charli may choose to disclose something important to Brittany.
  • Brittany’s response should be:
  • steady
  • kind
  • unsurprised in some ways
  • careful with Charli’s dignity
  • not saintly, not shocked, not melodramatic

Governing principle¶

Locked canon for current planning

Brittany earns trust not by demanding truth, but by handling ambiguity with grace.

4.5 Fiona¶

Locked canon (limited but usable)

Fiona is in a position where her respect matters socially and professionally. She is part of the group whose regard Charli has earned through competence.

At present, Fiona serves as evidence that Charli’s teaching mode is legitimate and valued.

4.6 Lauren¶

Locked canon

Lauren is part of the wider women-led network and has previously featured in emotionally significant scenes. She is associated with steadiness, survival, and practical recovery.

She belongs to the broader female competence network around Wardrobe.

4.7 Mara¶

Locked / broader canon

Mara is part of the women’s competence network and is linked to training, professionalism, and Charli’s status change. She is dry, standards-led, and non-sentimental.

Mara’s evolution in how she names Charli signals status change and metamorphosis.

5. Relationship Canon¶

5.1 Celeste ↔ Charli¶

Locked canon

This is a central relationship of the story world.

It includes: - love - guidance - female authority - emotional dependence / reassurance - placement and identity-shaping - asymmetry of strength without cruelty

Charli sees Celeste as home. Celeste can still stabilise Charli across distance.

5.2 Charli ↔ Brittany¶

Locked / current-growth canon

This relationship is becoming central in The Deep End.

It is built on: - mutual respect - Brittany’s groundedness - Charli’s admiration of Brittany’s practical knowledge - increasing trust - Brittany’s observational intelligence

This relationship should develop through earned trust, not pressure, surveillance, or forced confession.

5.3 Charli ↔ wider girls group¶

Locked canon

The girls’ inclusion should feel: - ordinary - warm - socially real - not predatory - sometimes accidentally pressuring simply because ordinary girlhood has bodily and social expectations

The danger in scenes like the pool-party scene should come from normality becoming difficult, not from the girls becoming villains.

6. Backstory and Deeper Continuity¶

6.1 Earlier hormonal / developmental history¶

Locked canon

Charli’s development includes: - very late male puberty - self-medication with spironolactone as a private “solution” to late puberty - spironolactone halting masculinisation and contributing to subtle feminisation - later supervised HRT approximately three months afterward

This history contributes to why she is now physically read as female.

6.2 Social vulnerability¶

Locked canon

Though physically read as female, Charli lacks much of the accumulated experience cis girls gain through adolescence. This creates vulnerability in ordinary social situations, especially where the body becomes socially foregrounded.

6.3 “Wife” label¶

Locked canon

The term “wife” for Charli originally came from Celeste casually assigning the role. Celeste did not initially understand how impactful the label would be. Other women heard it, agreed, and reinforced it, further shaping Charli’s emerging identity.

7. Voice, POV, and Style Rules¶

7.1 General prose values¶

Locked canon

Preferred prose qualities: - grounded - emotionally realistic - sensory-first where appropriate - concise rather than baggy - elegant but not purple - strong subtext - little to no melodrama

7.2 Character voice consistency¶

Locked canon

  • Celeste: 1st person, past tense, crisp, perceptive, controlled
  • Sarah: acerbic, dry, standards-driven
  • Charli: emotionally sincere, observant, vulnerable, not over-articulate when distressed
  • Brittany: practical, decent, perceptive, grounded

7.3 Tone guardrails¶

Locked canon

Avoid: - thesis-heavy exposition - explaining what the scene already embodies - sentimentality that outruns character truth - turning social pressure into spectacle - making bodily difficulty prurient

Aim for: - emotional truth - dignity - precise social pressure - women-led steadiness

8. Established Recent Scene Canon¶

8.1 The phone-call hinge scene (recent)¶

Locked canon

Scene function¶

This scene is complete and should stand as a short hinge scene.

It accomplishes: - reassurance for Charli - reassurance for Celeste - a reader-helpful articulation of privacy, trust, and earned disclosure

Key idea established¶

Locked canon

Charli should not tell Brittany out of fear. She should wait until Brittany has become someone she trusts, then decide what, if anything, Brittany is entitled to know.

Emotional ending¶

Locked canon

Charli expresses that she feels home with Celeste. Celeste replies with assurance:

“You are, petal. That will never change.”

This line lands as reassurance for both of them.

8.2 Structural significance¶

Locked canon

This short scene resolves a larger concern: Celeste’s fear that Charli might not survive being away from the Wardrobe container. The scene proves that phone contact has become a viable support mechanism.

9. Scene Five Canon Planning¶

9.1 Core movement¶

Locked canon for planning

Scene Five should move from: - one week of earned ease and growing competence - into Friday-night social relaxation - into a casual suggestion of a pool party - into Charli’s sudden bodily / social alarm

9.2 What the scene is about¶

Locked canon for planning

The pool-party scene is not primarily about exposure or Brittany “figuring it out.”

It is about: - ordinary inclusion becoming practically difficult - the collision between being wanted and not being able to enter the invitation simply - Charli’s need to navigate ordinary girlhood when it becomes embodied

9.3 Beat structure¶

Locked canon for planning

  1. Bridge / earned ease
  2. Friday-night release
  3. Casual pool-party suggestion
  4. Internal calibration / silent plunge
  5. Social pressure distribution
  6. First deflection
  7. Brittany quietly helps / pressure adjusts
  8. Exit with unresolved aftershock

9.4 What to avoid¶

Locked canon for planning

  • no voyeuristic emphasis on the body
  • no cruel-girl caricatures
  • no Brittany becoming interrogative too soon
  • no humiliating collapse of Charli’s competence

10. Scene Six Canon Planning¶

10.1 Prerequisite¶

Locked canon for planning

Scene Six disclosure only works if Brittany has earned trust in Scene Five.

10.2 Necessary conditions¶

Locked canon for planning

Scene Six should be built on: - Brittany having shown consistency and kindness - Charli deciding, not being driven - disclosure being selective and self-possessed - Brittany responding as a decent young woman, not a saint and not a shock machine

10.3 Thematic logic¶

Locked canon for planning

Celeste’s lesson was not “don’t tell.” It was “don’t tell out of fear.”

So Scene Six should become: Charli tells Brittany out of trust.

10.4 Important language distinction¶

Locked canon

Avoid framing disclosure as “no secrets between real friends,” because that risks implying total disclosure is morally required.

Prefer: - private truth entrusted - selective trust - “I wanted you to hear this from me” - “I trust you”

11. Continuity Watchlist¶

These points should be kept in foreground while drafting.

11.1 Reader safety / reader trust¶

Locked canon

Because many readers may be trans women or otherwise personally invested in Charli’s situation, scenes should avoid teaching: - that danger is everywhere and inevitable - that ordinary difficulty is proof of doom - that privacy is shame - that disclosure is owed to earn kindness

Instead, scenes should model: - discernment - earned trust - dignity under pressure - realistic hope

11.2 Female moral atmosphere¶

Locked canon

Women in this world should be strong, competent, and often right. Even where they are imperfect, the narrative centre remains aligned with female intelligence and structuring force.

11.3 Compression vs underdevelopment¶

Provisional craft note

Short scenes are not inherently slight. If a scene resolves a genuine emotional or structural problem, brevity can be a strength. Do not pad scenes simply to make them look “substantial.”

13. Useful Governing Principles¶

(Quick Reference)

For Celeste scenes¶

  • name the feeling accurately
  • separate uncertainty from actual danger
  • restore agency
  • do not soothe lazily

For Charli scenes¶

  • preserve dignity
  • let vulnerability remain intelligent
  • avoid making panic look foolish
  • let belonging and pressure coexist

For Brittany scenes¶

  • perceptive, not invasive
  • kind, not performative
  • grounded, not idealised
  • trustworthy because she handles ambiguity well

For the story overall¶

  • women structure the moral weather
  • risk exists, but is not the whole truth
  • trust is earned
  • privacy is not shame
  • hope must remain realistic

16. Chronological Timeline of Past Events (Working Continuity Sequence)¶

16.1 Pre-story / formative background¶

Charli’s earlier developmental history¶

Locked canon

  • Charli entered male puberty very late.
  • In private distress over late puberty, she began self-medicating with spironolactone, treating it as a “solution.”
  • This halted masculinisation and contributed to subtle feminisation.
  • Roughly three months later, she began fully supervised HRT.
  • As a result, she is now physically read as female and is effectively indistinguishable from a cis girl her age.

Implication¶

Locked canon

Charli’s social risk profile is that of a young woman, but without the full lived apprenticeship most girls accumulate through adolescence.

identity shaping¶

16.2 Earlier social / identity shaping¶

Celeste’s naming influence¶

Locked canon

  • The term “wife” for Charli originated casually with Celeste.
  • Celeste did not initially understand how consequential that label would become.
  • Other women heard it, accepted it, and reinforced it.
  • This contributed to Charli’s social and emotional placement.

Female containment model¶

Locked canon

  • Charli’s growth took place within a women-led structure where standards, competence, and female interpretation guided her.
  • Wardrobe functioned as a powerful container for belonging, placement, and behavioural shaping.

16.3 Celeste’s Girl period (broad continuity)¶

Provisional summary canon

This period establishes: - the central Celeste–Charli bond - Charli’s increasing alignment with womanhood - female guidance as formative rather than decorative - Wardrobe / women-led environments as morally and socially structuring forces - the asymmetry between Celeste’s decisiveness and Charli’s responsiveness

Important continuity note¶

Locked canon

The story is never only “about Charli becoming someone else.” It is equally about: - Celeste’s influence - women’s authority to name and place - the social reality of being shaped inside a female world

16.4 Wardrobe-era deepening¶

Locked / broader continuity

  • Charli came to be known, held, and interpreted correctly inside Wardrobe.
  • She did not need to hide there in the same way she does elsewhere, because the women around her already knew how to place her.
  • This is crucial to later scenes: what she misses outside Wardrobe is not merely affection, but a knowing female buffer.

16.5 Transition into The Deep End¶

Locked canon

  • The story expands beyond Wardrobe proper into the Queensland / Maleny setting.
  • This creates separation from the full Wardrobe container.
  • Charli must now function more independently in a fresh social environment.
  • Celeste has serious concern about whether Charli can cope away from the full female structure that has held her.

16.6 Early The Deep End concern state¶

Locked canon

  • Charli enters a new environment where her belonging is not yet fully settled.
  • Celeste worries that sending her there may prove too risky.
  • Ordinary female social dynamics are now potentially harder because Charli no longer has constant interpretive shelter.

16.7 First week in Queensland / Maleny¶

Locked current continuity

  • The first week passes relatively uneventfully.
  • Charli moves into teaching mode and loves it.
  • Her usefulness and competence stabilise her.
  • She earns respect from everyone from Fiona on down.
  • Sarah is largely occupied with optimising the physical plant and is not in Charli’s close orbit.
  • Brittany does not probe further, but remains in quiet observation mode.
  • Some things Brittany notices do not reconcile neatly with “tomboy.”

16.8 The Celeste / Charli phone hinge scene¶

Locked canon

  • Charli worries about what she may owe Brittany once Brittany starts noticing rough edges.
  • Celeste clarifies that not all uncertainty is danger.
  • Celeste gives the governing principle:
  • Charli does not owe panic-driven disclosure.
  • Trust must come first.
  • Charli emotionally reveals how deeply she still experiences Celeste as home.
  • Celeste reassures her: “You are, petal. That will never change.”

Structural consequence¶

Locked canon

This scene reassures: - Charli, that she still has shelter in Celeste - Celeste, that Charli can still be steadied across distance - the reader, that separation is survivable because support still exists

16.9 Scene Five setup¶

Locked planning canon

  • It is now Friday night.
  • Charli has had nearly a week of provisional ease.
  • She is socially included and beginning to breathe.
  • A social outing with the girls leads to a casual suggestion of a pool party.
  • This becomes the next major embodied test.

16.10 Scene Six setup¶

Locked planning canon

  • If Brittany proves herself safe in Scene Five, Charli may choose in Scene Six to tell her something private.
  • This should occur because Brittany has earned more of Charli, not because Charli has been pressured into confession.

17. Core Character Dossiers (Working Draft)¶

17.1 Charli dossier¶

Status¶
